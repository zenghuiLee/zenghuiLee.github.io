Humans of China│人在中国（九十一）

发表于 Dec 2, 2018

Humans of China
人在中国 Exploring China one person at a time 一次探索一个中国人

From： Cameron Hack CJHphotography

I came on the train from Guangdong to Beijing in 1957 and when I first arrived I really didn’t like it here. There was too much noodles and not enough rice and coming from the south of China where rice is our staple food it was hard to get used to mainly eating them. I moved to Beijing as my husband was a soldier, he had just finished his time in North Korea and the government asked him to return to Beijing to work. He has since died and now I live alone in one of the hutongs, however my children often visit me and I have good friends here so I am not lonely. I’ve seen great changes in Beijing, all for the best and now life is much better and I don’t mind living here. In 1962 I was really missing my hometown and wanted to go back home for a visit. So, with 2 kids we boarded a train with 1 ticket for a hard seat and set off for a 2 day train journey. There were no seats for my kids so I had to hold them whilst they sat on my lap. The ticket was around 30 or 40 yuan but I might be mistaken. At night when they fell asleep I’d place them under the chair, lying down until they woke up the next morning. Flying on an airplane was not a luxury people like us could enjoy,needless to say, at the time, I was just happy to be going home. I haven’t been home for around 4 years now and this year I am 82 so I don’t know when or if I’ll have the chance to go back. I don’t have much there anymore as most of my family have passed away but I still really like it there, especially being close to the sea. My mum lived until she was 90 and my grandma was 96 when she died but they were my adopted family. My real mother gave birth to 4 girls but my grandma didn’t like girls, she liked boys so my mum gave me and one of my sisters to another family to raise. I was felt pretty sad about the fact she gave me away but my adopted family who treated me very well even though my dad wasn’t around much as he was often in Indonesia for business.

Beijing

1957年的时候，我第一次坐火车从广东来北京，我刚来的时候很不喜欢北京。这里吃的很多都是面条，没有什么米饭，因为我是南方人，米饭是我们那里的主食，所以主食是面条很不习惯。我搬来北京主要是因为我丈夫是军人，他在朝鲜服完役后政府让他回北京。他已经去世了，我现在一个人住在胡同里，但是我孩子们经常来看我，而且我有很多好朋友，所以不觉得孤单。我看到北京发生了很大的变化，变得越来越好，现在生活比以前好多了，我也不介意住这里。1962年的时候，我特别想我老家，想回家看看。所以我带我两个孩子上了火车，但是我们只有一张车票，很难找到座位，就这样我们坐了两天的火车才到家。当时我的孩子没有座位，我必须得抱着他们坐在我膝盖上。火车票当时大概30－40块一张，具体记不清了。晚上，他们睡着了我就把他们放在座椅下面，让他们在那里睡觉，第二天等他们醒了后再抱出来。他们睡的很不舒服，但是没有别的选择，我们也没有飞机可以坐，但是能回家我还是很开心。我已经四年没有回家了，今年我82岁，我不知道自己有没有机会再回家看看。我老家那里也没有特别多留下的，我的家人大多已经过世了。我妈妈一直活到90岁，我奶奶一直活到96岁，但是她们都是我领养家庭的妈妈奶奶。我自己亲生妈妈生了四个女儿，但是我亲生奶奶不喜欢女孩子，她喜欢男孩子，所以我妈妈把我和我另一个姐妹送人了。她把我送人这件事我觉得挺好的，因为我的领养家庭对我很好，但是爸爸经常不在家，他经常在印度尼西亚出差。

北京
